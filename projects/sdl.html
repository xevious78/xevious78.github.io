"The Lights' Show"

10 weeks to create a 50 minute-long Light & Music show on the walls of my university. (2015).

A bit of Context

The Challenge

Decibels is an association that sets up and controls the light and sound hardware needed for live shows. I had the chance to take part in this group in many different ways: as a secretary, a live sound engineer, and as a studio sound engineer.


One day, the leader of the light team had the crazy idea of creating a light, music, theatre and dance show using the walls of our university. I immediately accepted to take part in this challenge !

Script Writing

The Story

The Theme

Being students of an engineering school, the question of technics was a strong pivot of our life that we absolutely wanted to share with the inhabitants.


I proposed the idea of using light as a metaphor of technics. Light appears at the beginning of the show along with the discovery of fire. It then evolves across the history of the human-kind shaped by technics.

We named our challenge "The Story of Impossible":
How can a team of 10 people create and organise such a big show in less than 10 weeks ?

Music Composition

Let's compose the "Story of the Impossible".

Music was among the central components of this show, however we didn't have much time to compose and record everything.

So we decided to focus on Ambient Sound and Electronic Music compositions while asking the University's Orchestra to play well-known musics.

My Compositions

Industrial Era

The Industrial Era is a dark Ambient creation mostly made ouf of metallic sounds and machine noises. It starts with a frightening focus on coughing colliers followed shortly by the awakening of the beast: the steam machine that tirelessly creates objects by absorbing the hard work of miners.


The second part pictures the start of a steam train, a friendly monster the has been accepted by the human-kind as a new tool to shorten the world.


The third section contrasts with the mining phase by focusing on the variety of tools available rather than on the workers. It shows how the human-kind is ready to build anything above the surface of the Earth, no matter how hard it is.


The fourth and final component of this creation is a pessimist ending to the Industrial Era. It represents how the mastering of our tools led us to the creation of deadly weapons. This is a nice transition for the next phase of the show: The War.

Internet Era (Daftpunkouchette)

The Internet Era is an Electronic Dance Music made for a unicycle choreography. It pictures how new technologies brought valuable connections between people while pushing the human-kind to create things faster. Acoustic instruments are used throughout the music to show that technology should always rely on people.

Sound Setup

Microphones and Loudspeakers to rule them all.

We obtained professional sound gear to amplify the music played by the orchestra as well as our compositions. More than 20 high-quality microphones, 4 loudspeakers and 2 subwoofers were used during the show.



Light Setup

More than 50 light systems.

Here is the light patch we created: about 50 high-quality lights to connect and control, one professional video projector and one light tracer.



Final Result

We managed to create in less than 3 months an hour-long show that brought light, music, dance, circus and theatre together during three consecutive days.
